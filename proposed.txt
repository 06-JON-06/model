				<h2>
					CGH Group
				</h2>
			</div>
			<p win="-80,10,26,0,180,0,1500,2000">
				The CGH Group engages in research on 3D display using computer-generated holograms.
				Its ultimate goal is to develop 3D computer display and 3D TV technology to enable stereoscopic viewing of objects as shown on the right.<br>
				Computer-generated holograms are unique among the various types of 3D display available
				because they require no special glasses and fully suit the physiological conditions necessary for stereoscopic human vision.<br>
				However, a variety of related technical challenges still need to be addressed, and members of the laboratory are working hard to resolve them.
			</p>
			<br><hr width="100%" color="ffffff"><br>
			<h2 Dpos="-198,15,26" Drot="0,180,0">
				Computer-generated Holograms
			</h2>
			<p win="-80,10,26,0,180,0,1500,2000">
				Computer-generated holograms are one of holograms, whose interference fringe are generated by computer simulation,
				and have attracted attention as a 3D display technology that enables ideal 3D rendering.<br>
				For details, see the <a href="http://cgh.ist.hokudai.ac.jp/eng/cgh/holography_principle.html">Principles of Holography</a> and <a href="http://cgh.ist.hokudai.ac.jp/eng/cgh/cgh_principle.html">Principles of CGH</a> web pages.
			</p>
			<br><hr width="100%" color="ffffff"><br>
			<h2 Dpos="-192,15,35" Drot="0,180,0">
				Motivation behind the Pursuit of Computer-generated Hologram Development
			</h2>
			<p win="-80,15,75,0,45,0,1500,2000">
				Various types of <a href="http://cgh.ist.hokudai.ac.jp/eng/cgh/3d-display.html">3D display</a> enable stereoscopic image rendering.
				However, only those based on the principles of holography suit human visual function requirements for the recognition of stereoscopic structures and objects.
				Accordingly, computer-generated holograms are seen as a promising technology for ideal 3D image display in the future.
				Source: Opening article by Associate Professor Yuji Sakamoto entitled <a href="http://cgh.ist.hokudai.ac.jp/eng/cgh_age.html">"Now is the Age of CGHs" (in Japanese) in HODIC Circular Vol. 22, No. 2 (published in May 2002)</a>
			</p>

	<div id="content_title" Dpos="172,18.3,74.1">
		<h2>
			Computer-generated Holograms
		</h2>
	</div>
	<div id="content" Dpos="9.6,11,131.9" Drot="0,-45,0">
		<p>
			Holography is a 3D image display technology invented in 1946 by physicist Dr. Dennis Gabor (the winner of the 1971 Nobel Prize in Physics).
			It is theoretically the ultimate form of 3D display, and produces images that are seen as indistinguishable from the original object or scene.
			Holograms generated by computer simulation are called computer-generated holograms (CGH).
		</p>
	</div>
